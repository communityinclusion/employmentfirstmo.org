The Department of Developmental Services, in partnership with the Institute for Community Inclusion / UMass Boston presents:

Spring 2014 Training for Massachusetts Employment Support Professionals

As DDS moves forward with implementation of it's Employment First policy it is important to afford professionals the opportunity to acquire the knowledge and skills necessary to deliver quality integrated employment and day services. This year the ICI is offering two options for training for employment support professionals. The six session comprehensive series incorporates best practices in career planning, job development, job coaching and support. The second option provides providers the option to select just those sessions that meet their needs at this time, though it should be noted that Foundations of Community Employment is a prerequisite for the other workshops.

Comprehensive Employment Supports Training Series

This six-day, 36-hour training series incorporates state of the art practices in career planning, job development, job coaching and support. This series includes six full-day sessions that will be held between April and May. It is expected that registrants will attend all training days.

Foundations of Community Employment (1 Day)

This workshop begins with an overview of community employment (past and present), including current social, political, and economic changes that are leading to expansion of opportunities for individuals both in MA and nationally. The focus will then turn to collaboration with the variety of stakeholders who may influence and support the employment process. Topics here will include building relationships and trust, keeping people engaged in helping, and communicating effectively.

Career Planning: A Person-Centered Approach (1 Day)

This workshop will focus on strategies used to assess the job seeker's employment goals, abilities, interests and capacities. Participants will learn how to coordinate and implement person-centered planning, identify personal and family networks, develop vocational profiles and schedule and evaluate situational assessments.

Job Development And Business Engagement (2 Days)

Developing job opportunities and finding the right job match in an ever-changing economic environment places increasing demands on employment professionals. This two-day workshop focuses on understanding employer needs and developing quality job matches. Topics include employer networking; marketing to employers and responding to objections; identifying unmet business needs; dealing with disclosure; and job carving and creation.

Job Coaching And Support Strategies (2 days)

This two-day workshop focuses on job training and support strategies. Topics include the role of the job coach; natural supports and social inclusion; systematic instruction basics; maintenance and fading; and behavioral support strategies.

Employment Support Training Workshops

This option offers the same content as the Comprehensive Series, but with the flexibility for trainees to take the workshops separately. This training approach is designed for staff who may have more specialized roles, as either job developers or job coaches, or for staff who have participated in some training in the past and need the opportunity to develop skills in a specific area.

While these workshops can be combined, trainees interested in all four workshops are encouraged to register for the Comprehensive Series.

  • Trainees may take Foundations of Community Employment only.
  • Trainees must take Foundations of Community Employment as a prerequisite if they intend to take any of the following workshops:
    • Career Planning: A Person-Centered Approach
    • Job Development and Business Engagement
    • Job Coaching and Support Strategies

Certification

Both the Association of Community Rehabilitation Educators (ACRE) and APSE offer certification for employment professionals.

ICI is an approved ACRE training provider. Participants will have the opportunity to earn a Basic ACRE Certificate of Achievement in Employment Services if they attend all six days of training detailed above, as well as complete additional required fieldwork, and take additional training regarding Social Security Work Incentives.

Anyone interested in obtaining an Employment Specialist Certificate through participation in this training should view the archive of the ACRE Certification Webinar (held on March 25, 2014) for more information.

There will be an ACRE sign-up sheet on the first day of training, and ACRE fieldwork packets will be made available at that time as well.

APSE has established the Employment Support Professional Certification Council to oversee the first national certification program for employment support professionals. To earn the designation of Certified Employment Support Professional, one must pass a national exam. For more information go to http://www.apse.org/certification/. Note that ACRE continues to work collaboratively with APSE to insure that training offerings are aligned to APSE's standards.
