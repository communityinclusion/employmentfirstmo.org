The Department of Developmental Services, in partnership with the Institute for Community Inclusion / UMass Boston presents:

The Road to Employment and Purposeful Community Involvement through Community Based Day Supports

November 13 & 14 2014

This “how to” presentation and working session will offer providers practical guidance on how to develop and operate quality CBDS programs that offer people the opportunity to explore career opportunities, gain work experience and develop meaningful relationships in their communities. Particular focus will be given to the importance of structure and scheduling, staff training and support as well as quality assurance and accountability. Participants will leave the training with preliminary plans for program development or improvement within their agencies.

Who should attend: Individuals responsible for program development and transformation within their agencies. Participants should plan to attend both days of this training.

Dates: Thursday/Friday, November 13 & 14, 2014
Time: 9:00-4:00 with registration and networking starting at 8:30.
Continental breakfast and lunch will be provided.
Cost: $40
Location: Hampton Inn, 319 Speen Street, Natick

Presenter: Genni Sasnett is currently an independent consultant specializing in community based supports and employment for individuals with developmental disabilities. As the COO of St. John’s Community Services. Genni led the transformation of that agency as it moved from traditional facility based services to becoming a leading provider of employment and community based supports for individuals with intellectual and developmental disabilities in the Washington DC area. She is currently a subject matter expert for the US DOL’s Office of Disability Employment Policy and a consultant with the Institute for Community Inclusion. In 2012 she received the Service Award from AIDD for her work as a leader at St. John’s Community Services and for the work she has done to improve service delivery models and quality of life for individuals with disabilities and their communities.
