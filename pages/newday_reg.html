--- layout: reg_layout title: A New Day - Creating Pathways to Employment and Meaningful Days ---

This one-day training presented by Sara Murphy, Program Director at Transcen will introduce participants to the WorkLink model. A wrap around approach to employment and day supports, this model has led to employment outcomes and meaningful days for individuals with significant barriers to employment. This flexible, hybrid approach allows support plans to be "built to order". Employment is the program's primary goal. Day services are focused on the discovery process, exploring vocational options and building employment skills. Participants also use day services for non-work needs and to augment part-time work schedules. Sara will discuss the braided approach to services and management of a 100% community based, individualized program that is currently being replicated in a number of locations in California.

This training is designed for executive directors, program managers and others who have responsibility for program development. Attendees are encouraged to bring their own devices (laptop, tablet and/or smart phone) for use during this training.

Presenter: Sara Murphy,

Sara Murphy, M.S.Ed. is the director of TransCen, Inc.'s WorkLink Program, a San Francisco-based employment service for job seekers with disabilities. For more than 25 years, Ms. Murphy has been involved in program development and system change. She has helped to established innovative transition and employment programs throughout the country and abroad. Sara is also a key contributor to TransCen's consultation team where she provides training and technical assistance to government agencies, school districts, service providers and advocacy groups. She is regarded as an expert presenter and unique trainer. Because of her direct service experience, she is able to "operationalize" Employment First, customized employment and other best practices. Her trainings provide professionals with field-tested strategies and tools. Ms. Murphy is an active participant in a number of consortiums and taskforces at a local, state and federal level. She has supported many of TransCen's federal contracts, such as SSA's Youth Transition Demonstration Projects and ODEP's Customized Employment projects. Ms. Murphy is also a subject matter expert for ODEP's Employment First Community of Practice and a consultant for Cincinnati Children's Hospital's Project SEARCH program where she is an ad-hoc trainer for the Project SEARCH Training Institute.
