Online Training: Foundations of Community Employment (January 5 – 30, 2015)

This workshop begins with an overview of community employment (past and present), including current social, political, and economic changes that are leading to expansion of opportunities for individuals both in MA and nationally. The focus will then turn to collaboration with the variety of stakeholders who may influence and support the employment process. Topics here will include building relationships and trust, keeping people engaged in helping, and communicating effectively.

Event management for Online Training: Foundations of Community Employment powered by Eventbrite

Upon completing this training, students will be able to:

  • Understand historical, social & political factors leading to expansion of community employment
  • Explain the fundamentals of Title 1 of the ADA re: employment for people with disabilities
  • Explore models & quality indicators for delivering employment services
  • Consider the variety of stakeholders involved in achieving employment success
  • Discuss principles of effective collaboration

This workshop will be available around the clock (24/7 from January 5th through January 30th. It should take about six hours to complete. Participation is self-paced and flexible. Included are a variety of formats: readings, presentations, discussion forums, videos, professional development activities, and assignments. An instructor will be available on a regular basis throughout the month to answer questions and give feedback.

Webinar: Awesome Apps and Adaptations – Achieving Vocational Success for Workers with Intellectual Disabilities (January 13)

The iPad and the iPhone are profound tools for individuals who experience various disabilities. Recently, this tool has been used in creative ways to support vocational success among individuals who experience intellectual disabilities. This webinar will discuss apps for self-regulation, work task sequencing, work task prompting, executive function, as well as amazing adaptations and accessories to support the worker.

After completing this webinar, participants will be able to identify:

  • 3 ways to use the built in camera for creating “swipe and tap job coaching"
  • 3 apps for self regulation in the workplace; 3 apps for executive function; and three apps for remembering to perform specific work tasks
  • 3 body worn adaptations that can be made to the iPAD
  • 3 must have accessories for the iPad and how to use them in the workplace.

Webinar: Low Cost Work Site Accommodations in Minutes (January 22)

This webinar will explore over 50, low cost, worksite accommodations that can be made in minutes using every day tools and materials, to support workers who experience intellectual disabilities.

After completing this webinar, participants will be able to identify and describe:

  • work place accommodations using colors for task completion prompts
  • 3 workplace accommodations for job task sequencing
  • 3 types of tape for task stabilization and securing
  • 3 types of materials for creating workplace jigs and fixtures to reduce cognitive demands related to task completion.
